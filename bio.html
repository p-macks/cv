Bio

After finishing my A levels, stuck on exactly what was the way forward, with little career guidance and resources back then in the rural areas Filabusi i had a choice to either join the impoverished Zimbabwean civil service or apply for an apprenticeship (a government subsidized skills learning programme).
With little effort succeeded in being part of an apprenticeship with an engineering group which specialized in maintenance of hydraulic and pneumatic plant machines. Passing my theory in Machineshop engineering also was a walk in the park and the nightmare started when failed to get fulfilliment in the practical aspect of my job. My foreman and other colleagues were satisfied with my work but personally couldn't and that's when thought a career change would do justice to situation.
While at the maintenance shop came across CNC machines and being fascinated by their speed in producing a month's workload in a few hours made me curious. My programming desire started and since then have always been inspired by ideas that makes everyday human lives easy
